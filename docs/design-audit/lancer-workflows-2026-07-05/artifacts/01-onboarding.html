How someone actually uses it

Trust, then pair, then everything else

A

See the product before trusting it

One dark proof card (a machine, a pending approval, a finished diff) sits above the headline, then the pairing card — lancerd pair instruction, 6-digit field, help link. No feature-row carousel competing for attention.

B

Wrong or expired code

The typed digits stay on screen; the error moves from a muted center line to a red, field-adjacent sentence, plus a "still stuck?" recovery line naming the fix (lancerd pair again).

C

Notifications, right after pairing

Asked immediately after the machine relationship works — mechanism-critical, not an engagement nicety. Concrete example copy plus Turn on / Maybe later. — Lancer account vs. self-hosted offline — asked only once the machine relationship is established, not before.

D

Policy defaults, one tap

Balanced pre-selected with Continue with recommended; Customize opens the three presets (Balanced, Always ask, Fast lane — three plain-language risk defaults, changeable later in Settings.

E

Account, optional and last

Skippable offer after pairing and alerts are understood. Local/self-hosted mode needs no account; recovery and second-device sync are the only reasons to add one now.

iOS 27 / WWDC 2026 — what applies here

Pairing is the first trust grant, not a peripheral setting

Cross-checked against the full platform research pass; only findings that concretely touch a first-run pairing moment are included below.

AA

Pair Machine as an authenticated intent, from day one

IntentAuthenticationPolicy = .requiresAuthentication · WWDC26 Session 347

Apple's own agentic-security session names device authentication as the deterministic mitigation for actions that change what an agent can do. Pairing is the single highest-trust moment in the whole app — it grants a machine access, not just removes it — and is a stronger first candidate for this policy than almost any other Lancer intent.

OC

Camera-read codes, if QR ever comes back

OCRTool / BarcodeReaderTool · WWDC26 Session 241

On-device vision tools can now read a barcode or on-screen digits directly during model generation. If the deferred QR/camera path is revisited post-V1, this removes the need for a bespoke scanner — point the camera at the machine's code and let the model transcribe it, rather than building custom OCR.

NT

Re-test the pre-prompt promise against new grouping

Notification coalescing + urgency-based grouping · iOS 27

Notifications now coalesce per-app and get reordered by on-device urgency rather than strict chronology. Panel E's promise — "Lancer only interrupts when needed" — should be re-verified on a real iOS 27 device once shipped, since system grouping could reorder Lancer's own priority ahead of or behind other apps' alerts.

Full source doc: docs/design-audit/2026-07-05-ios27-wwdc26-platform-capabilities.md — none of these three block V1.
